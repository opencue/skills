# Preset: op-ed

**Reads like**: FT comment / Stratechery / Matt Levine without the jokes.
**Length**: 800-1400 words.
**Voices**: none (single author voice). `--voices` is ignored for this preset.

## Structure

```
1. Title (H1, ≤70 chars) — a claim, not a question. Take a position.

2. Thesis paragraph (one paragraph, 80-130 words)
   - State the claim in the first sentence.
   - Give the one supporting reason in the next two sentences.
   - Telegraph the structure of the argument that follows.

3. Argument paragraphs — 3-5 paragraphs, each:
   - 110-180 words.
   - Opens with the sub-claim as the topic sentence.
   - Supports with ONE concrete piece of evidence (named entity, named number, named source) {#source}.
   - Closes by tying back to the main thesis.

4. The strongest counter — one paragraph (100-150 words)
   - Acknowledge the best argument against the thesis.
   - Don't strawman it — write it as it would be made by an intelligent opponent.
   - Then explain why the original thesis still holds (or qualify it honestly).

5. Implication paragraph (one paragraph, 80-130 words)
   - Given the thesis is right, what should the reader do / believe / watch?
   - Concrete, not "more research is needed."

6. Closer line — one or two sentences.
   - A turn of phrase that crystallizes the thesis.
   - Memorable, but earned by the argument above.
```

## Voice rules

- Use "I" sparingly — once or twice in the whole piece. The thesis carries the voice; the pronoun is a crutch.
- Use the author's specific evidence (data they have access to, observations from their work). Generic claims read as weak.
- Adjectives are taxed. Verbs are free. Lead with the verb.

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ "In this article, I will argue that..." → start with the argument.
- ❌ "Let me explain why..." → just explain.
- ❌ "Many people don't realize..." → who are these people, and how do you know.
- ❌ Burying the thesis in paragraph 4 → it goes first.
- ❌ A "balanced" conclusion that pulls the thesis back → don't write op-eds you don't believe.
